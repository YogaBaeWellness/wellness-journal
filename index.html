What this is

This is not a checklist.
It is a practice.

Wellness For The Soul is a 30-day devotional journal created for the woman who is ready to slow down, breathe, and let God meet her exactly where she is. Not after she has it figured out. Not when things calm down. Right now.

"The yoga classes, the breathwork, the scripture — these are tools to get people through the door. What they leave with is transformation. That is what this journal is built to deliver."

— Shae, Founder of Yoga Bae Wellness Studio

Each day begins with 60 seconds of intentional breathwork to settle your nervous system before your mind engages. From there, you move through a curated Bible verse, a personalized prayer, guided reflection prompts that connect the Word directly to your real life, three intention lines for your relationship with God, and a whole-person self-check covering your mental, physical, and spiritual wellbeing.

The 30-day journey

A sequenced spiritual arc —
not random verses.

These 30 days were intentionally ordered to take you somewhere. Each week builds on the last.

Days 1–7

Arrive

Come as you are. Be still. Let God meet you exactly here.

Days 8–14

Surrender

Release your plans. Lean fully into trust. Pray beyond yourself.

Days 15–21

Be Held

Faith through the wait. Endurance. Healing. Anchored in love.

Days 22–28

Transform

Renew your mind. Become. Step into what God has prepared.

Days 29–30

Walk in Promise

Your gifts come from God. She believed — and she was blessed.

Inside every day

Six steps. Two pages.
A complete daily practice.

Breathwork Prompt

Set a 60-second timer. Inhale for a count of four, exhale for a count of four, getting completely empty. Arrive before you engage.

Today's Verse

A personally selected New Living Translation scripture, sequenced to match exactly where you are in the 30-day journey.

A Prayer

An original prayer written specifically to accompany the verse — personal, conversational, and designed to feel like a real moment with God, not a recitation.

Reflect

Two guided prompts that connect the Word directly to your actual life — then generous journaling space to go as deep as you need.

Intentions

Three lines to set meaningful, God-centered intentions for the day — not tasks, not goals. Commitments.

Self-Check

A 1–5 rating scale and guiding prompt for each dimension — mental, physical, and spiritual. Honest awareness of the whole person.
